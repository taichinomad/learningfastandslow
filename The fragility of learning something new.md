Finding out that even for things I truly want to learn did not survive the year. For things that I somewhat want to learn, stood the test of time because I had external structures pushing me to learn, rather than relying on my own fragile willpower. For go game, I had a weekly class schedule, joining competitions, attending workshops, and more recently, influencing co-workers to play the game too. For tai chi, it was more or less the same. Weekly classes, teaching people, workshops, competition (when I was much younger). It seems like these things have longevity because of external structures. And once I pass the period where I can start doing to a reasonable standard, motivation plays less of a factor. Wait, that’s not true. Even when I pass the point of reasonableness, external factors that promote showing up still stands. Look at running. When i stopped joining weekly classes, my consistency dropped. My weight increased. No amount of internal motivation pushed me to the next level. I’m hovering. This is so true even for things that I like, what more for the things that I maybe like.

I guess protecting the initial stages by motivation alone is not enough. This must be coupled by external containers, until it becomes a habit. My language learning had an upcoming trip, anime that I watch, a daily gamification of learning via duolingo. All external factors. Internal motivation is indeed overrated.

> [[Internal interest chooses the path, external structure keeps you walking it.]]

So, the question is not “Do I really want this?”. The question should be:

> Am I willing to build or buy a container for this?

Because without that, the odds are stacked against it, no matter how poetic or meaningful the goal is. So future decisions become:

- Not “Should I learn sumi-e?”
- But “What is the container for sumi-e in my life?”

No container = hobby vapor.

So, if I were to give myself one operating principle for learning, it’ll be this:

> Do not start learning goals. Start learning containers.

Then let curiosity choose which containers you step into.

Not:

- “I will learn X.”

But:

- “I will join X class / cohort / group / schedule.”

And if no container exists yet, then maybe *it is not the season for that skill*. Not because you do not want it. But because your life cannot hold it properly right now.
